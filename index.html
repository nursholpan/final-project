PERSUASIVE DESIGN

Persuasive design elements are constantly present in our daily online interactions, particularly on social media. Features like likes, notifications, and clicking sounds are designed to keep you engaged with the platform, encouraging you to return repeatedly. In doing so, they track and analyze your behavior to hook you up even more. The main factors of persuasion are motivation, ability, and triggers.

MORE PSYCHOLOGICAL TECHNIQUES

Infinite Scrolling

The brain gets small dopamine hits with each scroll, encouraging continuous engagement without realizing the passage of time

Fear of Missing Out - FOMO

Constant updates and notifications make users feel they might miss out on something important or exciting. This fear drives people to check platforms frequently to stay “in the loop.”

Social Validation

Likes, comments, and shares create a feedback loop of social validation. These triggers make users crave more approval and interaction, fostering a sense of self-worth tied to online presence.

“It’s a social-validation feedback loop... exactly the kind of thing that a hacker like myself would come up with, because you’re exploiting a vulnerability in human psychology.”
- Sean Parker, Former CEO of Facebook

Dopamine-Driven Feedback
aka Positive Reinforcement

Social media platforms are designed to trigger the release of dopamine in users' brains, which is associated with pleasure and reward.

“The short-term, dopamine-driven feedback loops that we have created are destroying how society works. No civil discourse, no cooperation; misinformation, mistruth. And it’s not an American problem—this is a global problem.”
- Chamath Palihapitiya, Former VP of Facebook
